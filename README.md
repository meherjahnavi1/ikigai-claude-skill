# 🌸 Ikigai Claude Skill

> A free Claude Skill that helps you discover your *ikigai* — your Japanese "reason for being."

---

## What is Ikigai?

**Ikigai** (生き甲斐) is a Japanese concept meaning "reason for being." It's the sweet spot where four things overlap:

- ❤️ **What you LOVE** — activities and topics that bring you joy
- 💪 **What you're GOOD AT** — your skills and natural strengths
- 🌍 **What the world NEEDS** — problems you can help solve
- 💰 **What you can be PAID FOR** — work people will compensate you for

When all four align, that's your ikigai.

---

## What Does This Skill Do?

This skill turns Claude into a warm, reflective conversation partner that:

- Guides you through thoughtful questions across all four ikigai dimensions
- Surfaces tensions and overlaps you might not have noticed
- Helps you build a **personal ikigai map**
- Synthesizes everything into an **ikigai statement** — in your words, about your life

It's not a quiz. Not a personality test. A real conversation, at your pace.

## Installation

### Step 1 — Download the skill

Download the file below:

👉 [`ikigai.skill`](https://github.com/meherjahnavi1/ikigai-claude-skill/raw/main/ikigai.skill)

### Step 2 — Install in Claude.ai

1. Go to [claude.ai](https://claude.ai)
2. Click your profile → **Settings**
3. Navigate to **Skills**
4. Click **Upload Skill** and select the `ikigai.skill` file

### Step 3 — Start a conversation

Open a new chat in Claude and try any of these:

> *"Help me find my ikigai"*

> *"I'm feeling lost in my career, can you help?"*

> *"I want to explore what meaningful work looks like for me"*

Claude will pick up the skill automatically and guide you from there.

---

## How the Conversation Works

You can choose your pace at the start:

| Mode | Best for |
|------|----------|
| 🗺️ Quick map | You want a fast first sketch |
| 🔍 Structured deep-dive | You want to go dimension by dimension |
| 💬 Free exploration | You prefer open, flowing conversation |

By the end, you'll have a **personal ikigai statement** — a sentence or two that captures where your four dimensions meet.

## Example Conversations

Here's a taste of what a session looks like:

---

### Example 1 — Career crossroads

**You:** I've been a software engineer for 8 years but something feels missing. I don't know what to do.

**Claude:** That feeling of something missing after years of doing something well is really worth paying attention to. Let's explore it together through the ikigai lens...

*What do you find yourself doing outside of work that energizes you — even if it feels totally unrelated to your job?*

**You:** I mentor junior developers on weekends. I also write a small newsletter about tech ethics.

**Claude:** Those two things together are really telling. You're drawn to teaching and to the *meaning* behind technology, not just the building of it. Let's go deeper — when you're mentoring, what's the moment that feels most rewarding?

> **Ikigai discovered:** *Teaching others to think critically about the technology they build.*

---

### Example 2 — Feeling unfulfilled despite success

**You:** I run a successful marketing agency but I feel empty. Everyone tells me I should be happy.

**Claude:** Success and fulfillment aren't the same thing — and the gap between them is exactly what ikigai helps map. Can I ask: what parts of your work, even small moments, still feel genuinely alive to you?

**You:** Honestly? When I work with nonprofits pro bono. It's the only time I feel like the work matters.

**Claude:** That's not a small detail — that's a signal. You're good at marketing, and you can be paid for it, but right now it's only connecting with what the world needs when it's for causes you believe in. What would it mean to make that the center, not the side project?

> **Ikigai discovered:** *Using marketing skills to amplify missions that matter.*

---

### Example 3 — Starting from scratch

**You:** I'm 24, just graduated, have no idea what I want to do with my life.

**Claude:** Perfect time to explore this — before the world tells you who to be. No wrong answers here. Let's start simple: what's something you could talk about for hours without getting bored?

**You:** Honestly, nutrition and how food affects mental health. I'm obsessed with it.

**Claude:** That's a great starting point. Now — have people ever come to you for advice on this? Or noticed that you know more than the average person about it?

**You:** Yeah actually, all my friends ask me what to eat when they're stressed or anxious.

**Claude:** So you love it, you know it well enough that people trust you on it, and it clearly connects to something the world needs. The next question is: could you imagine being paid for it — as a coach, a writer, a researcher, a product creator?

> **Ikigai discovered:** *Helping people improve their mental wellbeing through nutrition and lifestyle.*
